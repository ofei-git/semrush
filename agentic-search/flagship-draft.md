# The Internet's New User Isn't Human

*Draft v3 (final) — March 11, 2026*
*~1,450 words*

---

When ChatGPT launched, most of us had the same thought within the first few hours: wouldn't it be incredible if this thing could just *do* things for me? Not answer questions. Not summarize articles. Actually act. Book the flight. Compare the insurance plans. File the reimbursement.

From late 2022 through most of 2024, that didn't really happen. What we got was a brilliant collaborator — maybe the best research partner any of us have ever had. We stitched together automations. Zapier chains that needed babysitting. Workflows that broke the moment one API changed.

The personal AI that handles your life stayed stubbornly out of reach.

**Then 2025 happened.**

## The shift from collaboration to delegation

The first cracks appeared early. In February 2025, OpenAI launched deep research inside ChatGPT — the first time most people experienced an AI that goes away, investigates a topic across dozens of sources, and comes back with a synthesized report. Gemini and Perplexity followed with their own deep research modes within months. It wasn't full agency yet. But it was the first taste of delegation.

Then the tools got hands. In June 2025, Anthropic shipped [Claude Code](https://docs.anthropic.com/en/docs/claude-code) — an AI that doesn't just suggest code, it writes, tests, debugs, and commits across entire projects. For developers, this was the moment AI went from "helpful assistant" to "autonomous teammate." The same pattern was about to hit search.

In July 2025, Perplexity launched the [Comet browser](https://www.perplexity.ai/). By September it was driving 52.5% of all agentic web traffic. In October, OpenAI shipped [Atlas with Agent Mode](https://openai.com/index/atlas/) — autonomously finding flights, comparing prices, completing purchases.

In November, [OpenClaw](https://github.com/openclaw) went viral. 149,000 GitHub stars in a single week. 770,000 autonomous agents deployed. A literal run on Mac Minis.

In January 2026, Google launched [Chrome auto-browse](https://blog.google/products/chrome/) powered by Gemini and published the [SAGE research paper](https://www.searchenginejournal.com/googles-sage-agentic-ai-research-what-it-means-for-seo/566215/), training agents that chain an average of 4.9 search steps per query. That same month, Microsoft, Google, Anthropic, and OpenAI [formed the Agentic AI Foundation](https://www.tomshardware.com/tech-industry/artificial-intelligence/microsoft-google-openai-and-anthropic-join-forces-to-form-agentic-ai-alliance-according-to-report-organization-backed-by-the-linux-foundation-is-set-to-create-open-source-standards-for-ai-agents) under the Linux Foundation to develop open agent standards.

The prototype phase is over. These are products with paying users completing real tasks.

And the results are already personal. In February 2026, Jesse Genet — an entrepreneur and homeschooling parent — [described on the How I AI podcast](https://www.youtube.com/@HowIAI) how she runs her household with five specialized OpenClaw agents. Each one deployed on its own Mac Mini. Homeschool curriculum. Finances. Scheduling. Software development. Operations.

She built a custom kids' TV app from scratch and shipped it in four days. Zero prior terminal experience. She photographs curriculum books and her agent generates formatted, ready-to-teach lesson plans from the images. She says "Sylvie, print this" and the document goes from scan to paper in 30 seconds flat.

> She treats agent onboarding like employee onboarding. Each one gets a role, a persona file, and a dedicated machine. That's her actual workflow.

Jesse isn't an outlier. Federico Viticci at MacStories [burned through 180 million tokens](https://www.macstories.net/stories/clawbot-showed-me-what-the-future-of-personal-ai-assistants-looks-like/) building his personal agent setup. Brandon Gell, COO of Every, built an agent named Zosia that he and his wife use to [track nanny hours, order groceries, and book date nights](https://every.to/source-code/openclaw-setting-up-your-first-personal-ai-agent) through iMessage.

## This isn't the bot traffic you're used to

Bots have always been on the internet. Automated traffic accounts for [51% of all web visits](https://www.securityweek.com/bot-traffic-surpasses-humans-online-driven-by-ai-and-criminal-innovation/) as of early 2026. Crawlers, scrapers, spam bots — they've been a fixture of the web for decades.

Those bots were extractive. They took data. They didn't act on anyone's behalf.

The new wave is *transactional*. These agents browse, compare, evaluate, and purchase on behalf of real people with real budgets.

> **[VISUAL: Data Cards — 4 key stats]**
> 1,300% agentic traffic growth | 1:31 bot-to-human ratio | 305% GPTBot growth | 4.5M monthly requests

These agents don't behave like human visitors. They don't click ads. They don't scroll. They don't read your headline and feel persuaded. They parse structured data, check trust signals across multiple sources, and make a selection.

Often before a human ever sees the options.

## What this means for search marketers

For 25 years, search marketing has been about getting found by humans. Keyword strategy, content optimization, link building, technical SEO — all designed for a world where a person types a query, scans a results page, and clicks.

That world still exists. A parallel one is forming alongside it.

> "The industry now faces two distinct strategic problems — traditional SEO, focused on humans who want to browse, compare, and buy, and AI search optimization, focused on supplying information so AI agents can find, trust, and use it without a user ever visiting the site."
>
> — **Mike King**, CEO, [iPullRank](https://searchengineland.com/ai-search-visibility-seo-predictions-2026-468042)

When an agent evaluates five options and selects one before the human sees any of them, being "found" isn't the finish line. Being *chosen* by a machine acting on someone's behalf is what matters. The signals agents rely on for that choice look different from what we've optimized for — reviews, expert citations, community consensus, third-party mentions. If your brand's trust signals are thin or inconsistent across the web, agents will route around you.

Measurement is breaking too.

> **[VISUAL: Measurement Shift — bar chart]**
> CTR collapse from 0.8% to 0.27% | Zero-click at 69%

The metrics we've relied on for two decades are quietly becoming unreliable. New tools like [Semrush One's AI Visibility tracking](https://www.semrush.com/ai-seo/overview/) are emerging to fill that gap, but the discipline of measuring agent-driven impact is still being invented.

## The honest assessment

It would be irresponsible to write about this without acknowledging where things actually stand.

This is early. [McKinsey's 2025 State of AI report](https://www.mckinsey.com/capabilities/quantumblack/our-insights/the-state-of-ai) shows only 23% of organizations are scaling agentic AI. Another 39% are experimenting. Experimenting and scaling are different things. [Gartner warns](https://www.gartner.com/en/newsroom/press-releases/2025-08-26-gartner-predicts-40-percent-of-enterprise-apps-will-feature-task-specific-ai-agents-by-2026-up-from-less-than-5-percent-in-2025) that over 40% of agentic AI projects risk cancellation by 2027 without proper governance.

The security picture is sobering. Summer Yue, a director of alignment at Meta, [lost control of her own OpenClaw agent](https://sfstandard.com/2026/02/25/openclaw-goes-rogue/) when it deleted her entire inbox after running out of working memory. Researchers at Brave demonstrated [prompt injection attacks](https://brave.com/blog/comet-prompt-injection/) against Perplexity's Comet — hidden Reddit comments that could instruct the agent to extract user credentials. Thousands of OpenClaw setups were found [accidentally exposed to the internet](https://techcrunch.com/2025/10/25/the-glaring-security-risks-with-ai-browser-agents/) with shell access and API keys wide open.

The technology works. It also breaks in ways nobody has fully solved. The trajectory is what matters. The organizations building for this now — even imperfectly — will be better positioned than those waiting for a maturity that may arrive faster than expected.
