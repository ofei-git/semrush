## Strategic Context

Andrew Warden is pushing agentic search as a priority that could inform Semrush's product positioning, messaging, and how the tools are built. This isn't just a content cluster — it's an opportunity to establish Semrush as the authority on how search is evolving from human-driven to agent-mediated.

The content lives as a main navigation experience on Semrush.com (like clicking "Agentic Search" in the top nav). Long-term, these pieces integrate as chapters in the A-to-Z AI Search guide built earlier in 2026.

## Through-Line

**The internet is gaining a new user — and it isn't human.**

Bots already account for 51% of web traffic. But this new wave is different. These agents browse, compare, evaluate, and transact on behalf of real people. They don't click ads, don't scroll, don't get persuaded by copy. They parse structured data, check trust signals across sources, and make decisions before a human ever sees the options.

## Key Data Points (Verified)

- Bot traffic: 51% of all web traffic in 2026 (Imperva)
- Agentic traffic grew 1,300% Jan–Aug 2025 (HUMAN Security)
- AI bot visit ratio: 1:31 human visits Q4 2025, up from 1:200 Q1 2025 (TollBit)
- GPTBot growth: 305% YoY (Cloudflare)
- CTR from AI platforms to publishers: dropped from 0.8% to 0.27% in 6 months (TollBit)
- Google SAGE: agents chain 4.9 search steps per query vs. 1.3-2.7 previously
- Gartner: 40% of enterprise apps will feature AI agents by end of 2026
- Gartner: 25% drop in traditional search volume predicted by 2026
- Deloitte: agentic AI market $8.5B in 2026, $35B by 2030
- McKinsey: 23% scaling agentic AI, 39% experimenting
- Perplexity Comet: 52.5% of agentic web traffic (overtook ChatGPT Atlas)
- OpenClaw: 149K GitHub stars in one week, 770K autonomous agents deployed
- Zero-click searches: 69% of all queries
- Google's info co-location finding: 35% of agent shortcuts (SAGE paper)
